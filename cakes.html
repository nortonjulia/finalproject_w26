Classic Vanilla Layer Cake

Tender, fluffy layers with a dreamy cream cheese frosting. Simple ingredients done really, really well.

⏱ Prep: 30 min 🔥 Bake: 28–32 min ❄️ Cool: 45 min 🎂 Serves 12 ⭐ Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cake flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp fine salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs, room temp
  • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whole milk, room temp
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • Frosting: 8 oz cream cheese
  • ½ cup butter, softened
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

💡 Click ingredients to check them off as you gather them!

Instructions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans, then line the bottoms with parchment circles. Having everything at room temperature is key for a smooth, even batter.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: Whisk together cake flour, baking powder, and salt in a medium bowl. Set aside. Using cake flour (not all-purpose) gives you a finer, more tender crumb.
  3. Cream butter & sugar: In a stand mixer on medium-high, beat softened butter and sugar until very pale and fluffy, about 5 full minutes. Don't rush this step — it's what gives the cake its light texture.
  4. Add eggs & vanilla: Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each. Add vanilla. Scrape down the bowl between additions.
  5. Alternate wet & dry: On low speed, add flour mixture in three parts, alternating with the milk mixture (milk + sour cream whisked together) in two parts. Begin and end with flour. Mix until just combined — a few streaks of flour are fine.
  6. Bake: Divide batter evenly between pans. Bake 28–32 minutes until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out with just a few moist crumbs. Don't overbake!
  7. Cool completely: Let cakes cool in pans 15 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack. Cool fully before frosting — at least 45 minutes. Frosting a warm cake is a recipe for sliding frosting disaster!
  8. Make frosting: Beat cream cheese and butter on medium until smooth. Add powdered sugar one cup at a time. Add vanilla and salt. Beat on high for 2 minutes until fluffy.
  9. Assemble: Place one layer on a cake stand. Spread a generous layer of frosting on top. Add the second layer. Frost the top and sides. Run a bench scraper around the edges for a smooth finish, or swirl for a rustic look!

🌡️ Why Room Temp Matters

When butter, eggs, and milk are all at room temperature, they emulsify together into a smooth, cohesive batter. Cold ingredients cause the fat to seize up and the batter to curdle or look lumpy — which leads to a denser, uneven cake. I leave my ingredients out on the counter for about an hour before baking. In a pinch: place cold eggs in warm (not hot) water for 10 minutes, and microwave milk in 10-second bursts until just slightly warm to the touch.

🥣 The Creaming Method

When you beat butter and sugar together, the sharp edges of the sugar crystals cut tiny air pockets into the butter. These air pockets expand in the oven, giving the cake its light, fluffy structure. After 5 minutes, the mixture should look almost white and very fluffy. If it still looks yellow and grainy after 2 minutes, your butter was too cold — let it warm up a bit and try again. This step cannot be rushed or replicated later in the recipe.

🎂 Getting Even Layers

Use a kitchen scale to divide the batter evenly by weight — this is more accurate than eyeballing. If your cake layers dome up in the center after baking, use a long serrated knife to level them once they're completely cool. Place one hand flat on top of the cake and saw gently through the dome. The flat side goes up when assembling (or down, on the bottom layer). Leveled cakes stack more cleanly, frost more easily, and look more professional.
