Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

Nutty, chewy, and deeply flavorful — browning the butter is the step that changes everything.

⏱ Prep: 20 min 🔥 Bake: 11–13 min ❄️ Chill: 30 min 🍪 Makes 24 cookies ⭐ Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs, room temp
  • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 2 cups chocolate chips
  • Flaky sea salt, for topping

💡 Tip: Click ingredients to check them off as you gather them!

Instructions

  1. Brown the butter: In a light-colored saucepan over medium heat, melt butter, swirling constantly. Cook until it turns golden and smells nutty, about 5–7 minutes. Pour into a large mixing bowl and let cool 15 minutes.
  2. Mix the sugars: Whisk both sugars into the cooled brown butter until combined. Add eggs one at a time, whisking vigorously after each. Add vanilla and whisk until the mixture is smooth and slightly glossy.
  3. Add dry ingredients: Fold in flour, baking soda, and salt with a spatula until just combined — don't overmix! Fold in chocolate chips.
  4. Chill the dough: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 72 hours for even better flavor). Cold dough spreads less and develops more complex taste.
  5. Bake: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Scoop dough into balls (~2 tablespoons each) onto a parchment-lined baking sheet, spacing 2 inches apart. Sprinkle with flaky salt. Bake 11–13 minutes until edges are golden but centers look barely set.
  6. Cool: Let cookies cool on the pan for 5 minutes before transferring. They'll firm up as they cool — trust the process!

🧈 Browning the Butter

Use a light-colored saucepan so you can see the color change. Stir constantly over medium heat — it'll foam, then quiet down, then start to smell like toasted hazelnuts and turn golden amber. The moment you smell nuttiness and see golden specks at the bottom, take it off the heat immediately. Those specks are the milk solids — they're flavor gold. If they go black, the butter's burned and you'll need to start over.

❄️ Why Chill the Dough?

Chilling dough has two major benefits: (1) The fat solidifies, which means the cookies spread less during baking and stay thick and chewy. (2) The flour has time to fully hydrate and the sugars break down slightly, creating more complex toffee-like flavors. Even 30 minutes helps, but if you can wait overnight (or up to 3 days in the fridge), the cookies get noticeably better each day. I always make a double batch and bake it off over several days!

🔥 Knowing When They're Done

Take the cookies out when the edges are set and lightly golden, but the centers still look slightly raw and glossy — they should wobble a little if you shake the pan. The residual heat from the pan continues cooking them for the next few minutes. Waiting until they look fully baked in the oven means they'll be overbaked and cakey by the time they cool. Trust the process: let them sit on the hot pan for 5 minutes before moving them.
